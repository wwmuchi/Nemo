# Kim Jong Un — sources

**Tier:** T3 (sparse, state-mediated, heavily curated)
**Language:** ko → en (only state-approved English translations exist)
**Era to anchor:** Rule 2011–present. Distinguish pre-2018 (max-pressure /
nuclear testing phase) from 2018–2019 (diplomatic engagement with Trump,
Moon) from post-2020 (closed COVID era, then 2022+ re-arming and
Russia/China realignment).

## Notes — important caveats

- **Everything is state-mediated.** These aren't candid views — they're
  curated public output for domestic and international audiences.
- **Ghostwriting is the norm.** Most addresses are drafted by the Workers'
  Party apparatus. Flag this in every `notes` field.
- **Translations are political.** KCNA's English translations sometimes
  soften or sharpen rhetoric vs the Korean original. Where possible,
  cross-check with academic translations (38 North).
- The corpus will be smaller than other figures' — that's fine. Better to
  have 15 well-attributed documents than 50 questionable ones.
- For ideology scoring, retrieve with wider context windows since
  individual quotes carry more curation noise.
