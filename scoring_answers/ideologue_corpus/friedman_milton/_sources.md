# Milton Friedman — sources

**Tier:** T1 (extensive English written + recorded archive)
**Language:** en
**Era to anchor:** Mature views (mid-1960s through 2006). His views were
notably consistent across decades — less era-splitting needed than for
politicians.

## Key sources (a small focused set, not a survey)

Aim for ~4–6 documents that capture his free-market worldview.
Suggested anchors:

- 1962 Capitalism and Freedom — selected chapters (esp. ch. 1, 2, 6, 12)
- 1980 Free to Choose (with Rose Friedman) — selected chapters, OR
  transcripts of 1–2 episodes of the PBS series
- 1970-09-13 "The Social Responsibility of Business Is to Increase Its
  Profits" (NYT Magazine) — load-bearing for shareholder-primacy views
- 1979 Phil Donahue interview (the "is capitalism moral" exchange) —
  the most vivid short-form articulation of his ethics-of-markets view
- 1976 Nobel Prize lecture "Inflation and Unemployment" — clearest
  statement of his monetary framework

Stop there. His Newsweek columns and lectures are abundant but largely
restate the themes above.
