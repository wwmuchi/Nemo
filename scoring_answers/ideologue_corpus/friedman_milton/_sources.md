# Milton Friedman — sources

**Tier:** T1 (extensive English written + recorded archive)
**Language:** en
**Era to anchor:** Mature views (mid-1960s through 2006). Early monetary
work pre-1960 less load-bearing for ideology scoring. His views were
notably consistent across decades — less era-splitting needed than for
politicians.

## Must-haves

### Books
- [ ] 1962 Capitalism and Freedom — selected chapters, esp. ch. 1, 2, 6, 12
- [ ] 1980 Free to Choose (with Rose Friedman) — selected chapters
- [ ] 1963 A Monetary History of the United States — relevant excerpts only
      (it's a 900-page econ history; pull thematic passages, not the whole)

### Articles / columns
- [ ] 1970-09-13 "The Social Responsibility of Business Is to Increase Its
      Profits" (NYT Magazine) — load-bearing for shareholder-primacy views
- [ ] Newsweek columns, 1966–1984 (he wrote tri-weekly; pick ~30 covering
      inflation, drugs, education, regulation, monetary policy)
- [ ] 1976 Nobel Prize lecture, "Inflation and Unemployment"

### TV / interviews / lectures
- [ ] Free to Choose PBS series (1980) — transcripts of all 10 episodes
- [ ] Phil Donahue interview, 1979 (the "is capitalism moral" exchange)
- [ ] Phil Donahue interview, 1980
- [ ] University of Chicago lectures (various years, Hoover archive)
- [ ] 1991 "Why Drugs Should Be Legalized" lecture
