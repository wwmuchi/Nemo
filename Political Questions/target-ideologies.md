# Target Ideologies — Mapping from Question Categories

This document maps each of the 17 question categories in `questions-to-review.md` to the ideologies they help distinguish. Use it to decide which final outcome labels the quiz should produce and which questions are most useful for telling those outcomes apart.

---

## 1. Religion & Morality (46 questions)

Ideologies these questions help detect:

- Theocrat / Religious Fundamentalist
- Religious Conservative (Christian, Muslim, Jewish, Hindu, etc.)
- Christian Democrat (moderate religious)
- Secularist / Laicist
- Militant Atheist / New Atheist
- Spiritual-but-not-religious
- Christian Nationalist
- Political Islamist

## 2. Family, Gender & Sexuality (12)

- Progressive / LGBTQ+ Activist
- Feminist (Liberal / Radical / Socialist)
- Traditionalist Family Conservative (pro-life, anti-LGBT)
- Gender-Critical (TERF / radical feminist)
- Anti-natalist
- Patriarchal Conservative

## 3. Environment & Climate (17)

- Green / Ecologist
- Eco-Socialist
- Degrowther / Primitivist
- Eco-Technocrat (pro-nuclear, lab-grown meat)
- Climate Denier
- Animal Rights Activist / Political Vegan
- Industrialist / Productivist

## 4. Immigration & National Identity (85 — largest bucket)

- Ethnic Nationalist
- Civic Nationalist / Assimilationist
- Sovereigntist
- Globalist / Cosmopolitan
- Open-Borders Advocate / No-Borders
- Multiculturalist
- Proletarian Internationalist (classical Marxist)
- Federalist (EU / world federalism) vs Anti-Federalist

## 5. Foreign Policy & International Relations (41)

- Imperialist / Neoconservative (interventionist)
- Pacifist / Non-violent
- Isolationist ("America/Italy First")
- Anti-Imperialist (anti-Western leftist)
- Military Hawk vs Diplomatic Dove
- Militarist (compulsory service, military caste)

## 6. Justice, Crime & Law Enforcement (16)

- Law-and-Order Conservative
- Police / Prison Abolitionist
- Libertarian Drug-Legalizer
- Surveillance Hawk (more police powers)
- Punitive Justicialist vs Due-Process Liberal

## 7. Education (2 — too few to discriminate much)

- State-Schools Statist vs Private/Voucher Advocate

## 8. Healthcare (3)

- Universal / Single-Payer Advocate
- Free-Market Healthcare
- Naturopath / Anti-Modern-Medicine

## 9. Technology, Media & Privacy (10)

- Transhumanist / Accelerationist
- Techno-Optimist
- Luddite / Primitivist
- Cypherpunk (absolute privacy)
- Security-State Citizen (pro-surveillance)
- AI-Supremacist (AI should rule)

## 10. Labor & Workers (8)

- Classical Marxist (workers own the means of production)
- Syndicalist
- Workplace-Democracy / Cooperativist
- Pro-Business Capitalist
- Reformist Pro-Worker Liberal

## 11. Economy & Markets (34)

- Communist
- Revolutionary Socialist vs Reformist Socialist
- Social Democrat (welfare + market)
- Free-Market Liberal / Liberist
- Anarcho-Capitalist
- Right-Libertarian
- Distributist (widely-spread ownership)
- Keynesian / Mixed Economy

## 12. Taxation & Welfare (2)

- Welfarist (strong social state)
- Anti-Tax Libertarian ("Taxation is theft")
- Charitable Conservative (private charity > state welfare)

## 13. Government, Authority & State Power (36)

- Authoritarian (strong-leader)
- Totalitarian
- Anarchist / Anti-Statist
- Minarchist / Night-watchman state
- Classical Statist
- Monarchist vs Republican
- Constitutionalist

## 14. Democracy & Political System (14)

- Liberal Democrat
- Direct-Democracy Advocate (referenda)
- Anti-Democrat / Technocrat
- Populist (left or right)

## 15. Civil Liberties & Individual Rights (13)

- Rights-Based Liberal
- Security-First Authoritarian
- Civil Libertarian
- Communitarian

## 16. Society & Culture (32)

- Cultural Progressive
- Cultural Conservative / Traditionalist
- Reactionary
- Multiculturalist
- Left-Identitarian
- Right-Identitarian

## 17. Uncategorized (62 — touches several topics)

- Pro-Life Bioethics vs Pro-Choice Bioethics (abortion, euthanasia, prostitution, genetic modification)
- Egalitarian (equality of outcome) vs Meritocrat (equality of opportunity)
- Revolutionary vs Reformist
- Welfare-Chauvinist (welfare only for natives)

## Recommended Final Outcome Labels (MECE Set)

A balanced ~15-label set the quiz could output as the primary ideology result:

1. **Communist / Marxist**
2. **Democratic Socialist**
3. **Social Democrat**
4. **Progressive Liberal** (US-style)
5. **Classical Liberal / Libertarian**
6. **Anarcho-Capitalist**
7. **Left-Anarchist**
8. **Traditionalist Conservative**
9. **Christian Democrat**
10. **Nationalist / Sovereigntist**
11. **Fascist / Authoritarian Right**
12. **Green / Eco-Socialist**
13. **Technocrat / Transhumanist**
14. **Populist** (left or right variant)
15. **Moderate Centrist**

### Optional Secondary Tags (non-political)

These can be attached as additional descriptors on top of the primary label:

- Religious / Secular / Atheist
- Vegetarian / Vegan / Omnivore
- Pacifist / Hawk
- Globalist / Localist
- Pro-tech / Anti-tech

## Suggested Next Step

Pick ~10–15 outcome labels from the list above. For each label, decide which 5–10 questions from `questions-to-review.md` are the most diagnostic. The diagnostic questions become the "core" set; the rest become tie-breakers or optional.
